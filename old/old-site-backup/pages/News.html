GW Listens: October 13th, 2015 

The Student Association successfully advocated for the GW’s first-ever Peer Support Program, GW Listens. The program will operate as an after-hours call center staffed by anonymous undergraduate and graduate student volunteers. The phone hotline will allow a student who is looking to speak to a well-trained peer about something they are going through to anonymously call in after hours. 

Advocacy for GW Listens began last year, under former SA President Nick Gumas and EVP Avra Bossov. Co-Directors of Health and Wellness Chris Evans and Cole Ettingoff, along with the insights of Dean Mark Levine and President Andie Dowd, helped bring this idea to fruition. 

Instituting 2016-2017 Fall Break: September 29th, 2015 

Senate Pro Tempore Thomas Falcigno (U-CCAS) and Academic Affairs Chairwoman Alyssa Weakley (U-GWSB) led an effort to institute a two-day Fall Break for the 2016-2017 academic school year. The two started meeting with administrators and drafting legislation last spring. 

The Fall Break is scheduled for October 24th and 25th, 2016. 

Expansion of Kosher and Halal Offerings on the Mount Vernon Campus: September 21st, 2015 

Senators Charlie Spirtos and Erika Feinman, along with Director of Mount Vernon Affairs Calla Gilson, expanded Kosher and Halal meal options for students living on the Mount Vernon Campus. They are currently working on accommodating and incorporating dietary needs and desires in campus dining. 

Gelman Library Closures: August 19th, 2015

GW Administrators announced that Gelman Library will remain open 24/7 in light of concerns from the Student Body. President Andie Dowd was involved in the conversation to maintain accessibility of the essential student resource. Full Hatchet Article can be found here.

 

Putting Emergency Resources on GWorlds: June 9th, 2015

President Andie Dowd and Executive Vice-President Casey Syron work with administration to have emergency resources to be on the back of GWorlds for the incoming GW Class of 2018. These numbers are GW Police/EMeRG, GW Medical Services, GW Mental Health Services, GW Sexual Assault Response Team, and National Suicide Prevention Lifeline. Full Hatchet Article can be found here.
