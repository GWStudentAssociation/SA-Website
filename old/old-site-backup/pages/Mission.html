Mission

The mission of The George Washington University Student Association is to further the interests and promote the welfare of all students at GWU. Our responsibilities are defined in four ways: 

Advocate: Whether it's lobbying for more accessible mental health resources, internship credit, or a fall break, we are first and foremost committed to representing the entire undergraduate and graduate population's interests. 

Allocate: The SA Senate Finance Committee is responsible for distributing $1,200,000 to student organizations on campus. 

Advertise: With every GW student being a member of the Student Association, our network reaches all of GW's 25,000 students. We're positioned to support student organizations by advertising and promoting programing and efforts.

Assist: We're students too--students who care about helping you have the best GW experience possible. Check out our resources for Colonials like our Syllabus Bank, follow us for updates on Twitter, or email us at gwsa@gwu.edu. Erika and Thomas will also announce office hours this fall.  

Structure

Executive: Led by President Erika Feinman, the SA Executive Cabinet’s responsibility is to represent and advocate on behalf of targeted student interests and areas. 

Senate: Led by Executive Vice President Thomas Falcigno, the SA Senate is comprised of 38 undergraduate and graduate Senators, representing every college within the university’s academic structure. Senators are divided into three committees: Academic Affairs, Finance, and Student Life. Their major responsibilities include advocating on current issues and appropriating funding to more than 400 student organizations. 

Judicial: The smallest of the three branches, the Student Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate disputes and determine constitutionality of issues between parties within the SA, the Joint Elections Committee, any and all funding disputes among registered student organizations, and any cases or controversies not specifically outlined in the SA Constitution.
