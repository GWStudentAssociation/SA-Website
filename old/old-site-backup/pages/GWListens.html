About

This year, the GW Student Association and the Division of Student Affairs announced GW Listens - GW's first peer support network. GW Listens will allow students to anonymously call and speak to a well-trained peer who is ready to listen and help. This peer support program focuses on active listening, empathy, and problem solving techniques, which can assist a student who is looking to speak to someone who might have experience with something they are going through. The program is currently accepting applications for the inaugural class of Volunteers. If you are interested in building better mental health resources on campus, the application to be a GW Listens Volunteer can be found here.

These students will operate the newly implemented peer support line, which allows a student who is looking to speak to someone about something they are going through to anonymously call in after hours. The first class of GW Listens Volunteers will be trained in the Spring by Mental Health Services Staff. The call center will be operational starting in the Fall.

For more information please contact Chris Evans, the Student Association Director of Health and Wellness.
